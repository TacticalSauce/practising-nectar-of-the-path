Nectar of the Path

Opening Chants

Refuge and Bodhichitta




།སངས་རྒྱས་ཆོས་དང་ཚོགས་ཀི་མཆོག་རྣམས་ལ།
SANG GYÉ CHÖ DANG TSOK KYI CHOK NAM LA
In the Buddha, Dharma and Supreme Assembly,

།བྱང་ཆུབ་བར་དུ་བདག་ནི་སྐྱབས་སུ་མཆི།
JANG CHUP BAR DU DAK NI KYAP SU CHI
I take refuge until enlightenment is achieved.

།བདག་གིས་སིན་སོགས་བགིས་པའི་བསོད་ནམས་ཀིས།

May the merit of my generosity and other virtuous deeds

DRO LA PEN CHIR SANG GYÉ DRUP PAR SHOK
Lead to Buddhahood for the welfare of all beings. (Repeat 3x)

ཚད་མེད་བཞི།
The Four Immeasurables

May all sentient beings have happiness and its causes.

May they be free from suffering and its causes.

NYÉ RING CHAK DANG NYI DANG DREL WÉ TANG NYOM CHEN PO LA NÉ PAR GYUR CHIK
May they dwell in great equanimity, free from attachment and aversion to those near and far. (Repeat 3x)

Supplication to the Dakpo Kagyü

Great Vajradhara, Tilo, Naro,

།མར་པ་མི་ལ་ཆོས་རེ་སྒལ་པོ་པ།

Marpa, Mila, Lord of Dharma Gampopa,

Knower of the three times, omniscient Karmapa,

Holders of the four great and eight minor lineages:

Masters of the profound path of Mahamudra—

I pray to you, Kagyü Gurus; I hold your lineage.

Please bless this meditator who cuts all ties to this life,

May I be unattached to praise and gain.

MEN NGAK TER GO JÉ PÉ LA MA LA
Please bless this meditator who always prays

May genuine devotion be born in me.

།ཡེངས་མེད་སྒོམ་གི་དངོས་གཞིར་གསུངས་པ་བཞིན།

Nondistraction is the main practice of meditation, as is taught.

Please bless this meditator who rests directly, without fabrication,

GOM JA LO DANG DREL WAR JIN GYI LOP
May my meditation be free from conceptual mind.

།རྣམ་རོག་ངོ་བོ་ཆོས་སྐུར་གསུངས་པ་བཞིན།
NAM TOK NGO WO CHÖ KUR SUNG PA ZHIN
The essence of thoughts is dharmakaya, as is taught.

CHI YANG MA YIN CHIR YANG CHAR WA LA

MA GAK RÖL PAR CHAR WÉ GOM CHEN LA
Please bless this meditator who manifests in unimpeded display:

།འཁོར་འདས་དབྱེར་མེད་རོགས་པར་བྱིན་གིས་རོབས།
KHOR DÉ YER MÉ TOK PAR JIN GYI LOP
May I realize the inseparability of samsara and nirvana.

།སྐྱེ་བ་ཀུན་ཏུ་ཡང་དག་བླ་མ་དང།
KYE WA KÜN TU YANG DAK LA MA DANG
In all lives, may I not be parted from my true guru,

།འབྲལ་མེད་ཆོས་ཀི་དཔལ་ལ་ལོངས་སོད་ཅིང་།
DREL MÉ CHÖ KYI PEL LA LONG CHÖ CHING
Forever enjoying the richness of Dharma.

།ས་དང་ལམ་གི་ཡོན་ཏན་རབ་རོགས་ནས།
SA DANG LAM GYI YÖN TEN RAP DZOK NÉ
Perfecting the qualities of the paths and levels,

།རོ་རེ་འཆང་གི་གོ་འཕང་མྱུར་ཐོབ་ཤོག
DOR JÉ CHANG GI GO PANG NYUR TOP SHOK
May I swiftly attain the state of Vajradhara.

མི་འགྱུར་རོ་རེའི་ཞབས་ལ་གསོལ་བ་འདེབས།
A Supplication to Mingyur Dorjé

གཉུག་མའི་གཤིས་ལས་མ་གཡོས་ཆོས་ཀི་སྐུ།།
NYUK MÉ SHI LÉ MA YÖ CHÖ KYI KU
Unmoved from the natural state, you are the dharmakaya.

ལྷུན་གྲུབ་རང་སྣང་དག་པ་ལོངས་སོད་རོགས།།

Taming beings by whatever means necessary, you arise as the rupakaya.

མི་འགྱུར་རོ་རེའི་ཞབས་ལ་གསོལ་བ་འདེབས།།

Great terton Mingyur Dorje, I pray to you.

Mandala Offering

SA ZHI PÖ CHU JUK SHING MÉ TOK TRAM
Sprinkling the earth with perfume and strewing it with flowers,

།རི་རབ་གིང་བཞི་ཉི་ཟླས་བརྒྱན་པ་འདི།

Adorning it with Mount Meru, the four continents, the sun and moon:

།སངས་རྒྱས་ཞིང་དུ་དམིགས་ཏེ་ཕུལ་བ་ཡིས།

I imagine this as a buddha realm and offer it

DRO KÜN NAM DAK ZHING LA CHÖ PAR SHOK
So all beings may enjoy this pure realm.

བཅོམ་ལྡན་འདས་ཀྱིས་བརྗོད་པ་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པའི་སྙིང་པོ།
The Heart of the Perfection of Wisdom Spoken by the Blessed One (The Heart Sutra)

Nectar of the Path Liturgy

  • Perform the nine-fold breath.

The Four Thoughts

Begin by cultivating a sense of delight in the qualities of the freedoms and riches:



MI LÜ YÖN TEN DU MÉ DAK NYI CHEN
Seeing its many wonderful qualities,

YIN PAR SHÉ NÉ GA TRO JÉ YI RANG
I rejoice and delight in this human life.

Instructions for Contemplating Precious Human Life

Next, contemplate the impermanent nature of phenomena:



NGÖ NAM MI TAK GYA TSÖ LAP JI ZHIN
Like waves in the ocean, all things are impermanent.

GANG JUNG DANG DU LANG TÉ DROK SU KHYER
I will accept whatever happens and make it my friend.

Instructions for Contemplating Impermanence

Contemplate the interdependence of causes and conditions:



GYU KYEN DZOM NA DRÉ BU NGÉ MIN PÉ
When causes and conditions come together, a result is sure to follow,

LEK TSOK ZHEN PEN LÉ LA GANG NÜ BÉ
So I will do my best to help others and engage in positive deeds.

Instructions for Contemplating Karma

Contemplate the dreamlike quality of the suffering of samsara:



DRO WÉ DUK NGEL TSO WO SEM KYI JÉ
The suffering of beings is mainly produced by the mind.

RANG NYI RANG GI CHING LÉ DRÖL WAR JA
I must free myself from my self-created bonds.

Instructions for Contemplating the Suffering of Samsara

Refuge and Bodhichitta

Bring to mind the Three Jewels, the guides who will free you from the ocean of samsara's suffering:



MI LU TEN GYI KYAP NÉ KÖN CHOK SUM
Three Jewels, undeceiving and everlasting sources of refuge,

DAK SOK TÖN PA LAM DANG DROK DZÖ CHIK
Please be the guide, the path, and companions for myself and all beings.

Imagine Buddha Shakyamuni is in front of you, representative of all the Buddhas and Bodhisattvas. His body (outer form) is the sangha, his speech is the Holy Dharma, his mind (wisdom) is the dharmakaya.

Then recite the following three lines as many times as you are able:



NAMO BUDDHAYA
NAMO DHARMAYA
NAMO SANGHAYA

Imagine that as you recite the refuge prayers before Buddha Shakyamuni, his compassion increases strongly. He melts into light and dissolves into you, your minds merging indivisibly.



DÖN DAM KYAP NÉ RANG LA DZOK PA TÉ
The ultimate refuge is perfectly complete within me,

SEM NYI DÉ SHEK NYING PO YÉ SANG GYÉ
For my mind is buddha nature, enlightened from the very start.

Next, meditate on loving-kindness and compassion by thinking of all the infinite number of beings as members of your own family, recognizing that they share your wish to be happy and free from suffering:



RANG NYI JI ZHIN DÉ DUK LANG DOR LÖ
I feel compassion for all the beings

YONG SU KHYAP PÉ DRO NAM NYING RÉ JÉ
Who, like me, want to be happy and free from suffering.

Generate bodhichitta, thinking to yourself, "I will practice on the path to bring all the infinite number of beings to the state of buddhahood."



KÜN KYANG NAM KHYEN GYEL WÉ GO PANG LA
To bring all beings to the state of omniscient buddhahood,

DAK GI GÖ CHIR LAM LA JUK PAR JA
I will now practice the path.

Guru Yoga

In the space before you, imagine your own guru in the form of Vajradhara or whichever buddha inspires you.



LA MÉ NGO WO SANG GYÉ KÜN DANG NYAM
Know me, O Guru.
In essence, you are equal to all the buddhas,

LHAK PAR DAK LA KA DRIN CHÉ WA KHYEN
But in the kindness you have shown me, you are even greater.

Then recite the following as many times as you are able:



LAMA KHYENO
Know me, O Guru.

Imagine that the guru melts into light and dissolves into you, your minds merging indivisibly.



DÖN DAM RANG YANG LA MÉ NGO WOR DZOK
Ultimately, I am the same as the guru in essence,

LOP MÉ RANG ZHIN SANG GYÉ KÜN DANG NYAM
For the nature of the student is the same as that of all the buddhas.

Meditation on Nature of Mind

To conclude, let the mind rest naturally, without meditating yet undistracted, in the state in which you, the guru, and the buddhas are indivisible in essence. Maintain the flow of the mind's true nature — ordinary, just as it is.



RANG DANG LA MA SANG GYÉ KÜN GYI TUK
My mind inseparable from the minds of the guru and all the buddhas,

YER MÉ MA CHÖ RANG BAP NYUK MÉ SHI
I rest naturally, without fabrication, in my basic nature,

MA YENG MI GOM ZO MÉ RANG GA MA
Not meditating and not distracted, in uncontrived ordinariness.

RIG PA RANG NYI RANG GI NGANG DU DEL
Awareness relaxes into itself.

Instructions for Meditating on Stillness, Movement, and Awareness
Instructions for Pointing Out that All Phenomena Come from Mind
  • Perform the shaking HA.

Dedication

With the buddhas of the three times and ten directions, along with their offspring, as your witnesses, dedicate the virtue to the welfare of others:



DAK GI DÜ SUM SAK PÉ GÉ TSOK DANG
I dedicate all the virtue I have gathered throughout the three times

GYEL DANG JANG SEM DRO WÉ GÉ KÜN DOM
Along with the virtue of the victorious ones, the bodhisattvas, and all beings,

JI TAR SANG GYÉ SÉ CHÉ NGÖ PA ZHIN
Following in the footsteps of the buddhas and their heirs,

KHA KHYAP DRO KÜN TEN DÉ TOP CHIR NGO
To the lasting happiness of all the infinite number of beings.

At the request of the students of the Tergar community, Mingyur Dorjé composed this during a meditation session in 2010 at St. John's University in the northern United States.

From the stormy waves of birth, old age, sickness and death,

།སིད་པའི་མཚོ་ལས་འགོ་བ་སོལ་བར་ཤོག
SI PÉ TSO LÉ DRO WA DROL WAR SHOK
From the ocean of existence, may all beings be free.

།འཇམ་དཔལ་དཔའ་བོས་ཇི་ལྟར་མཁེན་པ་དང།
JAM PEL PA WÖ JI TAR KHYEN PA DANG
As the courageous Manjushri understood,

།ཀུན་དུ་བཟང་པོ་དེ་ཡང་དེ་བཞིན་ཏེ།
KÜN TU ZANG PO DÉ YANG DÉ ZHIN TÉ
And just like Samantabhadra as well,

།དེ་དག་ཀུན་གི་རེས་སུ་བདག་སོབ་ཅིང།
DÉ DAK KÜN GYI JÉ SU DAK LOP CHING
I now dedicate all this virtue,

།དགེ་བ་འདི་དག་ཐམས་ཅད་རབ་ཏུ་བསྔོ།
GÉ WA DI DAK TAM CHÉ RAP TU NGO
Following their example, one and all.

།སངས་རྒྱས་སྐུ་གསུམ་བརེས་པའི་བྱིན་རབས་དང་།
SANG GYÉ KU SUM NYÉ PÉ JIN LAP DANG
By the blessings of the Buddha, master of the three kayas,

།ཆོམ་ཉིད་མི་འགྱུར་བདེན་པའི་བྱིན་རབས་དང་།
CHÖ NYI MIN GYUR DEN PÉ JIN LAP DANG
By the blessings of the truth of the immutable Dharma,

།དགེ་འདུན་མི་ཕེད་འདུན་པའི་བྱིན་རབས་ཀིས།
GEN DÜN MI CHÉ DÜN PÉ JIN LAP KYI
And by the blessings of the Sangha's unwavering aspirations,

།ཇི་ལྟར་བསྔོ་བ་སོན་ལམ་འགྲུབ་པར་ཤོག
JI TAR NGO WA MÖN LAM DRUP PAR SHOK
May these dedications and aspirations be fulfilled.

An Aspiration for the World

ན་མོ། མཁེན་བརེ་ཡེ་ཤེས་ཀུན་ལྡན་པའི།
NA MO KYEN TSÉ YÉ SHÉ KUN DEN PÉ
Namo Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha.

།སངས་རྒྱས་ཆོས་དང་དགེ་འདུན་རྣམས། །
SANG GYÉ CHÖ TANG GEN DÜN NAM
With all your wisdom, knowledge, and love,

བདག་སོགས་འགོ་ལ་བརེར་དགོངས་ནས།
DAK SOK DRO LA TSER GONG NÉ
Please care for me and all other beings

།དུས་ཀི་རྒུད་པ་སེལ་དུ་གསོལ། །
DÜ KYI GÜ PA SEL DU SOL
And dispel the many troubles of our times.

གཞི་ཡི་ཐེག་པ་ཐེག་ཆེན་དང་།
ZHI YI TEK PA TEK CHEN TANG
May all the masters who sustain the teachings

།རོ་རེ་ཐེག་པའི་བསྟན་འཛིན་ཀུན།
DOR JÉ TEK PÉ TEN DZIN KÜN
Of the Foundational, Great, and Vajra Yanas,

།བདག་གི་དིན་ཆེན་བླ་མ་རྣམས།
DAK KI TRIN CHEN LA MA NAM
And all my gurus, with their great kindness,

།སྐུ་ཚེ་མཛད་འཕིན་རྒྱས་གྱུར་ཅིག །
KU TSÉ DZÉ TRIN GYÉ GYUR CHIK
Live long and their enlightened activities flourish.

གཞན་ཡང་འཛམ་གིང་ཡུལ་འདི་ན།
ZHEN YANG DZAM LING YUL DI NA
May those who protect our world from misfortune,

།ཉམ་ཐག་སྐྱོང་དང་ཤེས་ཡོན་སེལ།
NAM TAK KYONG TANG SHÉ YÖN PEL
Who promote education and prosperity,

།འཁོར་ཡུག་དཔལ་འབྱོར་རྒྱས་བྱེད་པའི།
KOR YUK PEL JOR GYÉ CHÉ PÉ
And who care for our environment

།སྐྱེས་རྣམས་ནད་མེད་དོན་གྲུབ་ཤོག །
KYÉ NAM NÉ MÉ TON DRUP SHOK
Be healthy and achieve their aims.

གོ་ལའི་ཁོན་ཀུན་ཞི་བདེས་ཁབ།
KO LÉ KYÖN KÜN SHI DÉ KYAP
May peace and joy spread throughout the world.

།འཁྲུག་རོད་ཡམས་ནད་མེད་པར་ཤོག
TRUK TSÖ YAM NÉ MÉ PAR SHOK
May conflicts and epidemics cease.

།འཁོར་ཡུག་ཚ་དོད་ཞི་བར་ཤོག
KOR YUK TSA TRÖ SHI WAR SHOK
May global warming come to an end

།ཆོས་སིད་མཛའ་མཐུན་རྒྱས་པར་ཤོག །
CHÖ SI DZA TÜN GYÉ PAR SHOK
And may religious and political harmony increase.

མཐར་ཐུག་མཁའ་ཁབ་སེམས་ཅན་གི།
TAR TUK KA KYAP SEM CHEN GYI
Ultimately, may all beings throughout space

།རང་གནས་ཡེ་དག་ངོ་བོའི་ཆ།
RANG NÉ YÉ TAK NGO WÖ CHA
Manifest their essence of innate, timeless purity

།མཁེན་བརེ་ཡེ་ཤེས་མངོན་གྱུར་ཏེ།
KYEN TSÉ YÉ SHÉ NGÖN GYUR TÉ
And their wisdom, knowledge, and love.

།ཕམ་ཅིག་མངོན་སངས་རྒྱས་པར་ཤོག །
CHAM CHIK NGÖN SANG GYÉ PAR SHOK
May we all awaken together.

ཅེས་པ་འདི་ཡང་མི་འགྱུར་སྤྲུལ་མིང་པས་སི་ལོ་ ༢༠༢༢ ཟླ་ ༩ ཚེ་ ༢༠ ཉིན། སིགས་དུས་ཀི་མགོན་པོ་གུ་རུ་པདྨ་འབྱུང་གནས་ཀི་གནས་ཆེན་མཚོ་པདྨར་བྲིས་ཏེ་སོན་པ། འཛམ་གིང་ཞི་བདེས་ཁབ་པའི་རྒྱུར་གྱུར་ཅིག

This aspiration was written by Mingyur Tulku on September 9th, 2022 at Tso Pema, the sacred pilgrimage site of Guru Padmasambhava, our protector in these dark times. May it be a cause for peace and joy to fill the world!

ཡོངས་དགེ་མི་འགྱུར་རིན་པོ་ཆེེའི་ཞབས་བརྟན་གསོལ་འདེབས།
Long Life Prayer for Yongey Mingyur Rinpoche

མི་འགྱུར་ཆོས་དབྱིངས་སྤྲོས་པ་བྲལ་བའི་ཀློང་།
MIN GYUR CHÖ YING TRÖ PA DREL WÉ LONG
As the expanse of simplicity, the unchanging space of reality,

ཐུགས་ཀྱི་རྡོ་རྗེ་ཧེ་རུ་ཀའི་བདག་ཉིད།
TUK KYI DOR JÉ HE RU KÉ DAG NYI
Your enlightened vajra mind, embodiment of the Heruka,

དུས་མཐར་བླ་མའི་གཟུགས་ཀྱི་རྣམ་རོལ་པ།
DÜ TAR LA MÉ ZUK KYI NAM RÖL PA
Manifests in the form of the guru until the end of time.

འདྲེན་པ་མཚུངས་མེད་དེ་ཡི་ཞབས་བརྟན་གསོལ།
DREN PA TSUNG MÉ DÉ YI ZHAP TEN SÖL
Peerless guide, may your life be long!
